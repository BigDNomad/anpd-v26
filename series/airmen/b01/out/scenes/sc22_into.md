Vance pulled Archer back from the door by the harness strap and put his mouth close to his ear.

"Weight on the seat, not your feet. You stand on the penetrator you'll spin. Sit it like a horse and keep your knees in." He tapped the cable where it ran through the hoist mechanism. "Cable discipline. You don't grab the cable. You don't lean into it. It goes where it goes and you go with it."

Archer nodded.

"Abort signal is two hard tugs. You feel two tugs, you lock your arms around the shaft and you do not let go. Bird has to move, it moves with you on it. You understand me?"

"Yes."

"Say it back."

"Weight on the seat. Don't grab the cable. Two tugs, lock and hold."

Vance looked at him for a moment. Then he stepped back and let Meat take the hoist.

Meat ran the penetrator out to the door edge, checked the cable drum, checked the brake. He did it without looking at Archer. He'd done it enough times that looking wasn't part of it.

Archer clipped in, checked the clip, checked it again. He swung his legs out over the door sill and the air hit him, warm and thick with moisture, the valley floor a hundred and fifty feet below through broken canopy. He could see where the trees thinned near the crash slope. He could not see the survivor.

Meat released the brake and Archer dropped.

The penetrator swung once as the cable paid out, then steadied. The rotor wash pushed at him from above and the canopy came up slow. He watched the tree line. He watched the gap in the canopy where the descent path opened. He kept his hands off the cable.

He was forty feet down when the first shots came.

Not single shots. Not a probe. A line of muzzle flash opened from the eastern tree line and then the western tree line answered it and then from the south, lower and heavier, the RPD opened up with its distinctive tearing sound, longer bursts than the AKs, a different register entirely. Three axes. Simultaneous. The valley lit up with it.

The RPG came from the south tree line. It passed the penetrator close enough that Archer felt the pressure change before he heard it, a hard thump of displaced air, and then it was gone, detonating somewhere above and behind the helicopter with a flat crack that rolled back off the karst walls.

The penetrator swung hard left. Archer locked his knees and kept his hands on the shaft.

Above him the door guns opened. Animal's voice came through the rotor noise, not words, just the sound of someone working. The HH-3E was taking hits. He could hear them. Small caliber, punching through the fuselage skin, a sound like a staple gun on sheet metal, irregular, coming from two directions.

Archer was suspended in open air. No cover. Nothing between him and the tree lines but sixty feet of cable and the penetrator seat. He could see the muzzle flash from the eastern position clearly now, a shallow depression in the brush line, two shooters at least. The RPD had shifted its angle. It was walking rounds toward the helicopter.

He unslung the GAU-5/A.

He fired at the eastern position, short bursts, controlled, the carbine bucking against his shoulder while the penetrator swung under him. He shifted to the southern position. The RPD went quiet for four seconds and then started again.

Sandy rolled in from the north.

The Skyraider came down the valley at low altitude, rocket pods firing in sequence, and then the 20mm opened up and the southern tree line came apart. The RPD stopped. Two secondary fires started in the brush. Sandy pulled off hard left and the sound of the engine dopplered away up the valley.

The AKs kept firing.

Then the tail rotor took the hit.

Archer felt it before he heard it — a change in the helicopter's attitude above him, a yaw beginning, the nose swinging. The RPG detonation was behind the tail pylon and the sound was different from the first one, heavier, with a metallic secondary crack that meant something had connected.

Dalton broke right.

The HH-3E came off its hover hard, full pedal, and the cable went from vertical to a long arc in less than two seconds. Archer held the shaft with both arms, carbine swinging free on its sling, and the canopy came at him sideways. Branches hit his legs, his back, his shoulder. He was moving through the upper canopy at speed, the bird dragging him west through the trees, and there was no way to reel him in, no way to stop it, just the cable and the arc and the jungle coming through him.

The harness gave at the left shoulder buckle.

He felt it go. One second he was on the penetrator and the next he was falling, and the canopy caught him once and then again and then a third time, each impact slower than the last, branches breaking under him, and then the jungle floor came up and he hit it and did not move.

Above the tree line, Jolly Green Two-Two limped west. The canopy closed over the spot where he'd gone in. Vance stood at the door and watched it until there was nothing left to watch.
