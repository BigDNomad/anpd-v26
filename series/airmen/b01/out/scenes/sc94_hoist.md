The Jolly Green came in low over the tree line, nose up, tail rotor working hard against the hover. The rotor wash hit the canopy and the leaves went sideways and the whole jungle shook. Meat had the hoist arm swung out and the jungle penetrator already dropping before Archer and Coyle cleared the last of the undergrowth.

Coyle's weight was mostly on Archer's left side. The leg had stopped bending right somewhere back in the corridor and now it just dragged, the foot turned wrong. Archer had his arm around the man's ribs and was pulling him forward the way you'd pull a fence post out of soft ground, steady and without stopping.

The penetrator came down through the rotor wash, swinging in a slow arc, and Archer got his hand on the cable and pulled it in.

He looked at Coyle. Coyle looked back at him. The man's eyes were clear enough but his hands were shaking and there was no grip strength left in them. Archer could see that without testing it.

He wrapped himself around Coyle from behind, got his arms through Coyle's and locked his wrists together across the man's chest, then reached back and took the cable himself, threading it through the penetrator saddle and locking the bar across both of them. His weight and Coyle's together. He keyed the radio once.

Meat started the hoist.

They came up through the rotor wash and the jungle fell away below them and Archer kept his eyes on the tree line and not on the ground. Coyle's head dropped back against his shoulder. The man was still conscious. Just done.

Dalton had the door and he and Meat pulled them in over the skid and Archer got Coyle down on the deck and unclipped the penetrator and handed it back to Meat without a word. Meat had it back out the door and dropping before Archer had straightened up.

Below, Vance was running.

He came out of the tree line at a dead sprint, or what passed for one. His stride was right, his arms were right, and then the round took him in the left leg and he went down hard in the grass, twenty meters short of the penetrator still swinging on its descent.

Archer was at the door.

Vance was down and not moving and the penetrator was still dropping and the Pathet Lao were coming out of the jungle behind him, three of them, maybe four, moving fast with the tree line at their backs.

Sandy came in from the north.

The Skyraider was low and slow and the napalm canister came off the wing clean and tumbled once before it hit the ground at the edge of the tree line. The fireball went up orange and black and the concussion rolled across the grass and the wall of fire stretched forty meters across the line of advance and the men behind Vance stopped being men.

The rotor wash pushed the fire sideways for a moment and then it straightened and burned.

Vance moved.

He got his hands under him and pushed up and the leg didn't work right but it worked enough. He came up on one knee, then one foot, then both, and he was moving again, not running now but moving, the leg dragging some, covering the twenty meters to the penetrator in a straight line without looking back at the fire.

He got his hands on the cable. Got the bar across his chest. Keyed his radio once.

Meat brought him up.

The hoist motor ran and Vance came up through the rotor wash with his head down and his hands locked on the cable and the fire still burning below him in the grass. Dalton and Archer pulled him in over the skid and he went down on the deck next to Coyle and Archer got the penetrator off him and Meat swung the hoist arm in and Dalton got the door.

The Jolly Green nosed forward and climbed.

Archer looked at Vance's leg. The round had gone through the outside of the thigh, high up. It was bleeding but not the kind of bleeding that ended things. He got a pressure dressing on it and Vance held it there with his own hand and didn't say anything.

Coyle was on his back looking up at the cargo ceiling. His eyes were open. The fractured leg was straight now, or straight enough, and someone had put a folded jacket under his head.

The jungle fell away below the aircraft and the karst ridgelines came up gray in the early light, the sky above them going from black to the color of old pewter. The Mekong was out there somewhere to the west. Thailand beyond it.

Archer sat back against the bulkhead and let his hands rest on his knees.

The hoist arm was stowed. The door was closed. The fire in the grass was already behind them and getting smaller, and then it was gone.
