# CSAR — Scene Outline (Airmen, Book 1 of the new run)

<!-- Operator-authored outline. Scene-organized, 100 scenes, 1:1 with synopsis. Series: Airmen. Protagonist: Danny Archer. Co-lead (canon): Tom Coyle. Setting: 1969, Laos/NKP. Voice: Elmore Leonard. -->


## ACT ONE — THE CRASH AND THE SCRAMBLE

**Scene 1 - [NON-ACTION]**

Archer arrives at NKP — down the cargo ramp into the heat and rot; Vance meets him at the jeep, "Are you my pup?" Establishes both men, tone.

**Scene 2 - [NON-ACTION]**

Drive to PJ headquarters; Vance lays out the two-year program and Rule #1: Don't fuck up.

**Scene 3 - [NON-ACTION]**

Coyle pilots the AC-119K "Black Widow" home from a Trail sortie, whittling between passes; his young gunship crew gossips on the intercom. NOTE: the Black Widow's crew are Coyle's gunship crew — NOT the Jolly Green Two-Two rescue crew (Dalton, Evans, Meat, Sparky, Animal, Vance, Archer), who do not appear in the gunship and are not aboard. Refer to the gunship crew only by role (Coyle's co-pilot, the gunners, the sensor operator), never by the Jolly Green crew's names. Establishes Coyle, aircraft, tone.

**Scene 4 - [SUSPENSE]**

Black Widow hunting along the Ho Chi Minh Trail spots an NVA convoy — headlights threading the dark karst. The crew calls it; Coyle commits. Technical detail on the gunship's sensors and the night-hunt.

**Scene 5 - [ACTION]**

Coyle puts the Black Widow into a pylon turn at 2,000 feet; the crew loads and prepares the three M134 miniguns. Coyle sights through the targeting reticle in the side window, tilts the aircraft, squeezes — the Black Widow rains hell on the convoy, trucks exploding, NVA killed.

**Scene 6 - [ACTION]**

The convoy survivors scatter and a few guns fire back; Coyle works a second and third pass, the crew calling hits and corrections, until the convoy is destroyed. Out of targets and low on time, Coyle turns the Black Widow for home across the mountains of Laos.

**Scene 7 - [ACTION]**

NVA SAM team in the karst locks the gunship; SA-2 launch. The gunship crew jumps to battle stations; Coyle takes the controls from his co-pilot (the Black Widow's own crew — not the Jolly Green crew).

**Scene 8 - [ACTION]**

The missile detonates close; shrapnel rakes the aircraft, killing or wounding most of the gunship crew; Coyle takes a shrapnel wound to the thigh — a deep soft-tissue wound, NOT a broken femur (the bone is intact; the wound is serious and bleeds badly but leaves him able to move, hobble, and be helped along over the days ahead). (Coyle, the pilot — Archer is NOT aboard the gunship; he is at NKP and does not fly until the rescue launch in Scene 17.) Fire reaches the wing tanks.

**Scene 9 - [ACTION]**

Coyle rides the dying gunship down — never bail if you can crash it — into the canopy and slope. The Black Widow tears apart.

**Scene 10 - [SUSPENSE]**

Coyle wakes alone among his dead crew, burned and bleeding; drags himself toward the comms gear as flame climbs toward the wing tanks.

**Scene 11 - [SUSPENSE]**

Coyle reaches the KL-7; chassis can't be destroyed in time; he pries the eight cipher rotors free, pockets them, and drags clear as Lao voices approach. He lies still with his .38.

**Scene 12 - [NON-ACTION]**

Bounchanh hears the radio report of a successful SAM strike downing an American gunship in his territory. He moves toward it. Introduces the antagonist on his own ground.

**Scene 13 - [SUSPENSE]**

The Pathet Lao reach the wreck; Bounchanh recognizes the cipher machine and orders Private Khamla into the flames to retrieve it.

**Scene 14 - [ACTION]**

Khamla heroically drags the KL-7 chassis clear; the AC-119 explodes, killing him.

**Scene 15 - [SUSPENSE]**

Coyle hears the tanks blow, assumes the machine is destroyed, doesn't know about Khamla; believing he holds the keys, he limps into the jungle.

**Scene 16 - [NON-ACTION]**

Bounchanh examines the recovered KL-7 chassis, finds the rotor mounts empty — the pilot has them. He radios his headquarters; the HQ commander calls Hanoi to report the victory and chassis recovery. Hanoi calls Captain Tran Van Khoa, already in Laos, assigning him to retrieve the KL-7 and the rotors. Tran gathers his men and heads out in trucks.

**Scene 17 - [NON-ACTION]**

Archer can't sleep on the eve of his first mission; stares at the ceiling fan — the scramble klaxon goes off.

**Scene 18 - [NON-ACTION]**

The team assembles in the briefing hut pulling on gear; Dalton, Evans, the crew, both PJs.

**Scene 19 - [NON-ACTION]**

Briefing: downed AC-119 in eastern Laos, CIA contract pilot Coyle, significant Pathet Lao activity. They scramble into the dark.

**Scene 20 - [SUSPENSE]**

Night transit aboard Jolly Green Two-Two; Archer's nerves, Dalton's gallows humor on the intercom; they link with the Sandy escort and Bird Dog FAC. Radio reception already spotty over the mountains.

**Scene 21 - [SUSPENSE]**

Daylight at the crash site; Jolly Green threads the karst hunting the survivor as Bird Dog works a faint radio contact. Pathet Lao dug in around the perimeter.

**Scene 22 - [ACTION]**

Vance briefs Archer on the hoist; Archer is lowered on the penetrator. Ground fire erupts from three directions — small arms, RPD, an RPG that just misses.

**Scene 23 - [ACTION]**

Archer unslings his GAU-5/A and returns fire from the penetrator; the Sandy rolls in with rocket pods and 20mm, killing several Pathet Lao and knocking out a machine gun — not enough. The RPG's second round hits the tail rotor pylon; Dalton fights the controls and yanks the bird hard right out of the kill zone — Archer swings through the trees on the cable, carbine still in his hands.

<!-- TWIST 1 -->
**Scene 24 - [ACTION]**

Archer's harness fails; he drops; the canopy breaks his fall in stages; the GAU-5/A — still in his hands from firing — catches on a limb and is torn from his grip. He lands hard and does not move — dead or unconscious, we can't tell. Jolly Green limps home; Vance watches the canopy for any sign of his pup. Nothing but green.

**Scene 25 - [SUSPENSE]**

Archer's first minutes on the ground: he comes to, orienting, fighting panic, taking stock of his gear and fixing Coyle's relayed coordinates.


## ACT TWO — ALONE ON THE GROUND

**Scene 26 - [NON-ACTION]**

The damaged Jolly Green finally reaches the Thailand airbase after the long flight back and lands. Vance is furious, demands another helicopter to retrieve Archer and Coyle. The pilot says he'll do what he can — the decision isn't his.

**Scene 27 - [SUSPENSE]**

Navigation under pressure: a Pathet Lao security element sweeps the ridge between Archer and Coyle; he moves parallel and below, timing his steps to theirs. No shots. Proves his training.

**Scene 28 - [SUSPENSE]**

Archer crosses a stream under a static sentry; a held breath, a thrown handful of gravel as misdirection, a clean slip past.

**Scene 29 - [ACTION]**

Bird Dog spots movement; Sandy makes a low pass to draw fire and mark guns but can't shoot — too close to the Americans. The flyby buys hope.

**Scene 30 - [SUSPENSE]**

Archer finds Coyle by radio; Coyle is bleeding out — pulse thready. "Your blood type?" "O-Negative." "This is your lucky day." Archer starts the buddy transfusion: ties off, runs the line through a Y-junction, adds citrate. Twenty minutes in which neither man can move.

**Scene 31 - [SUSPENSE]**

Mid-transfusion, a Pathet Lao patrol passes within ten meters; Archer covers Coyle with the pistol and cannot fire — moving would tear the line out of both of them. The patrol moves on; the transfusion completes; Coyle's color returns.

**Scene 32 - [NON-ACTION]**

Coyle shows Archer the eight cipher rotors and explains what their capture would cost — MACV-SOG, Air America, Project 404. He proposes burying them. They cache the rotors at a riverbend boulder formation Archer can find again.

**Scene 33 - [SUSPENSE]**

Archer radios a coded transmission of the rotors' location to Bird Dog. Command now knows the cache.

**Scene 34 - [ACTION]**

Archer relays their intended move to the alternate LZ to Bird Dog. A Pathet Lao scout hears his voice and investigates; Archer kills the scout with his knife. It rattles him; Coyle says it never fully leaves you.

**Scene 35 - [ACTION]**

Sandy is hit by ground fire and forced to break off; trailing smoke, the Colonel nurses the failing Skyraider back across the border onto the Thai side and crash-lands in a paddy close to the Thailand airbase, the aircraft sliding through the muck. The Colonel flying Sandy survives and is picked up quickly — he is near the base, on friendly ground — and reaches the airbase for transport and a replacement aircraft.

**Scene 36 - [ACTION]**

A two-man Pathet Lao flank pair stumbles onto Archer and Coyle; Archer drops one with his .45, Coyle the other with the .38.

**Scene 37 - [NON-ACTION]**

Tran and his troops arrive at the Pathet Lao base camp and take charge. The Pathet Lao are uncertain — Tran's authority is Hanoi's, their commander Bounchanh is in the field. They obey but secretly radio Bounchanh. Tran, studying the map and the cache-report intercepts, begins to suspect the rotors were hidden, not carried.

**Scene 38 - [ACTION]**

Coyle-saves-Archer reversal: half-blind with pain, Coyle hears a weapon cocked a beat before Archer and pulls him down; the burst goes high. The bond now runs both ways.

**Scene 39 - [SUSPENSE]**

Coyle wants Archer to abandon him and carry the rotor location to safety; Archer refuses — his job is rescue. They argue in whispers under a rock shelf as a patrol passes overhead.

**Scene 40 - [SUSPENSE]**

Coyle is weakening and they need food and rest; they spot a Lao village. They risk a vacant hut; Archer slips inside and takes a bowl of rice.

**Scene 41 - [SUSPENSE]**

The lady of the hut returns, sees Archer — a tense beat — then nods and hands him a roasted fish. They eat in the jungle; the food steadies Coyle. A moment of grace among strangers.

**Scene 42 - [NON-ACTION]**

The Colonel arrives at the Thailand airbase by jeep and commandeers another Skyraider, ordering it armed with napalm in addition to guns and rocket pods. He orders the base commander to give the Jolly Green CSAR team another helicopter, cutting the red tape.

**Scene 43 - [NON-ACTION]**

The CIA commander in Thailand decides killing Coyle is necessary rather than letting him be captured — the covert information he carries, and the fact that he helped design and field-test the first gunship's classified modifications, which can't fall into Hanoi's hands.

**Scene 44 - [NON-ACTION]**

At the Thailand airbase, Vance loads a fresh medical ruck into the replacement helicopter as the crew preps it for launch; says almost nothing; the others give him space. The ground-bound mentor's helplessness turning to resolve.

**Scene 45 - [SUSPENSE]**

The Colonel's commandeered Sandy and the CSAR team take off and head back into the mountains; Sandy flies ahead at top speed.

**Scene 46 - [NON-ACTION]**

Tran/Bounchanh friction by radio: Tran (at camp, commanding) and Bounchanh (in the field) clash over authority — Tran wants the pilot and cargo intact and quiet; Bounchanh wants kill counts and downed aircraft.

**Scene 47 - [NON-ACTION]**

Bounchanh, off the radio, makes plain to his own officers that he'll run his territory his way regardless of the Hanoi man; we see his contempt for Tran's caution and the operational risk it sets up. The fracture hardens.

**Scene 48 - [NON-ACTION]**

Bounchanh alone with his maps and notebook; the blue hair ribbon; the count of downed aircraft. We learn what drives him — Ban Sopkhao — without him saying it.

**Scene 49 - [ACTION]**

Against Tran's order to keep things quiet, Bounchanh's hidden gun team opens up on the Bird Dog FAC; the slow observation plane jinks away. The Sandy — the Colonel's replacement Skyraider, now back on station — rolls in and strafes the gun position, killing some of Bounchanh's men. The ambush costs him for nothing. The friction turns operational.

**Scene 50 - [NON-ACTION]**

Aftermath: Tran furious at the exposure and waste; Bounchanh unrepentant — every American bird in his sky is a victory. The alliance fractures further.

**Scene 51 - [SUSPENSE]**

Witnessing the ambush and the way they're herded, Coyle and Archer piece it together: the Pathet Lao are keeping them alive and mobile as bait to draw American aircraft into the guns. They struggle with what that means.

**Scene 52 - [SUSPENSE]**

Archer and Coyle go to ground for a hard hour as a full column passes on the valley trail below; they take stock of the men and weapons moving past. The enemy presence is visibly larger.

**Scene 53 - [NON-ACTION]**

More enemy troops from the base camp arrive at Bounchanh's field position; he orders them to fan out and search for the American pilot. The net tightens.

**Scene 54 - [SUSPENSE]**

Archer realizes the Pathet Lao are tracking Coyle's blood trail; he takes them into a mountain stream and they hide under tree roots as the searchers wade in close. The patrol moves on.

**Scene 55 - [SUSPENSE]**

Once clear, Archer packs Coyle's leg with organic material to staunch the wound; the bleeding slows and the blood trail goes cold — they can move without leaving a path to follow.

<!-- TWIST 2 -->
**Scene 56 - [SUSPENSE]**

Through Bird Dog (reception breaking up), Archer receives the Thailand base commander's message: terminate Coyle rather than let him be captured. Archer absorbs what he's just been ordered to do — and what it would cost him to do it. He says nothing to Coyle. The weight of it settles over everything.

**Scene 57 - [ACTION]**

Pathet Lao troops close in and surround them; Archer realizes they're about to be taken. With capture imminent, he prepares to kill Coyle with his pistol — and can't. Coyle sees that Archer can't, and prepares to shoot himself. A held, terrible moment — then Sandy arrives and drives the Pathet Lao to cover. They escape across the ravine.

**Scene 58 - [SUSPENSE]**

Fog thickens as the sun sets — blessing and curse: it hides them, but the Jolly Green can't fly the mountains in heavy fog.

**Scene 59 - [NON-ACTION]**

The Jolly Green tries to reach them but must abandon the attempt in the heavy fog. Vance wants to rope down into the jungle on the hoist line and join Archer, but Sandy refuses. They'll try again at dawn.

**Scene 60 - [NON-ACTION]**

The sound of the helicopter fades into the fog and does not come back. It lands on both of them at once: no one is coming today. They will spend the night in the jungle, in the dark, with the Pathet Lao still hunting them — and Coyle is weaker than he was this morning. The hope that carried them all day drains out. They make a cold camp and wait out the longest night either of them has known.

**Scene 61 - [SUSPENSE]**

Night; Coyle needs food. While he sleeps, Archer forages. The jungle is creepy and dark — the fog has blotted out the moonlight.

**Scene 62 - [ACTION]**

A python, drawn by the blood, closes on the sleeping Coyle; Archer kills it with the knife at the last instant. "Problem solved." They eat.

**Scene 63 - [NON-ACTION]**

The next morning, Bounchanh is furious with his field commanders: overwhelming forces searching, still no captured Americans. He threatens them.

**Scene 64 - [ACTION]**

Climbing to escape a gaining patrol, a rain shower triggers a flash flood that sweeps them downslope toward the enemy; they catch a root 50 meters short, mud-caked as camouflage; the patrol climbs past.

**Scene 65 - [NON-ACTION]**

Coyle tries to convince Archer to leave him and retrieve the rotors — Archer moves faster alone, can be saved; Coyle has a few rounds and will manage. Archer refuses.

**Scene 66 - [NON-ACTION]**

Coyle tells Archer that if he won't leave, they have no choice but to go on the offensive — if the enemy is on the defensive, they can't chase. Archer is reluctant but agrees. The bait realization (Scene 50) pays off: they stop being prey.

**Scene 67 - [SUSPENSE]**

Mid-Act status: Coyle's wounds are slow-killing him; Archer keeps him moving ridge to ridge. Bounchanh has committed his battalion plus Tran's NVA recon company; LZ density too high for helicopters. They are alone.

**Scene 68 - [ACTION]**

They find Pathet Lao grenade boobytraps; they dismantle them and rebuild the components into traps of their own for the pursuers.

**Scene 69 - [ACTION]**

One of their rebuilt traps catches a pursuing squad — a sharp blast and confusion the two men exploit to gain ground. Archer's competence peaks.

**Scene 70 - [ACTION]**

Archer's tactical error: emboldened by the trap that scattered the last squad, he sets a deliberate ambush on a pursuing patrol instead of continuing to evade — he wants to bleed them, not just slip them. He picks a kill zone at a trail bend and waits. But he's misjudged the size of the force: what he took for a lone squad is the lead element of a much larger company. When he springs the ambush, the return fire is overwhelming, and a second Pathet Lao element moves to flank — putting the slow, wounded Coyle directly in the path of the encirclement.

**Scene 71 - [ACTION]**

The cost of the error: the ambush collapses into a desperate fighting withdrawal. Archer breaks contact under heavy fire, half-dragging Coyle out of the closing flank; Coyle takes a fresh wound getting clear. They escape, but barely, and only because Archer burns through most of their remaining ammunition covering the retreat. He knows it was his own hubris — he had a clean escape and chose to fight — and the guilt of nearly getting Coyle killed rides him into the final stretch.

**Scene 72 - [ACTION]**

The fog ambush: Coyle and Archer use their voices, calling to each other in English, to draw the Pathet Lao into a steep-sided valley. Fog thick. The enemy moves into the ambush; they spend most of their remaining ammunition killing the lead elements until the Pathet Lao retreat. Then they dash for the alternate LZ as the fog lifts.

**Scene 73 - [SUSPENSE]**

Archer and Coyle approach the alternate LZ. Archer radios Bird Dog; Jolly Green is on its way, within the hour.

**Scene 74 - [NON-ACTION]**

Coyle tells Archer he must retrieve the rotors. Archer resists; Coyle convinces him they cannot let the rotors fall into enemy hands at any cost. Archer reloads his .45 with a fresh magazine and presses it into Coyle's hands for close defense, then takes Coyle's .38 revolver for himself and sets off at a brisk pace back the way they came. (Archer travels far faster alone, unburdened by the wounded Coyle.)

**Scene 75 - [SUSPENSE]**

Jolly Green flies over the mountains toward the alternate LZ, Sandy escorting. Coyle stays put where Archer left him, holed up and waiting; Bird Dog circles above him, keeping watch and reassuring him over the radio that help is close and to sit tight.

**Scene 76 - [SUSPENSE]**

Exhausted, Archer reaches the hidden rotors and recovers them. He drinks from the stream and heads back — slower, soaked with sweat, but he won't quit.


## ACT THREE — CAPTURE AND RESCUE

<!-- TWIST 3 -->
**Scene 77 - [ACTION]**

Pathet Lao find Coyle and surround him. Bird Dog sees them and radios Coyle. Coyle fights fiercely with the .45 Archer left him, hoping they'll kill him — then the slide locks back, empty. He can't kill himself. He pulls his knife, but they're on him and stop him cutting his throat. Coyle is captured.

<!-- LOWEST POINT -->
**Scene 78 - [NON-ACTION]**

Archer returns to find Coyle gone. Bird Dog gives him the news. The hero's lowest moment.

**Scene 79 - [ACTION]**

Jolly Green lands for Archer. He gives Vance the rotors and says he won't leave without Coyle. Vance is furious — and secretly proud. Archer takes Vance's GAU-5/A, ammo, and grenades, and disappears into the jungle. Vance hands the rotors to the pilot, grabs another GAU-5/A, more ammo, two Claymores, and two sets of night-vision goggles from the bird's stores, and follows Archer in.

**Scene 80 - [NON-ACTION]**

Sandy curses when he hears what they've done — but thinks them brave. He orders Jolly Green to a safe area to set down, and tells Bird Dog to stop following Vance and Archer so as not to give away their position. All they can do is wait.

**Scene 81 - [SUSPENSE]**

Tran's Soviet-trained medic treats Coyle just enough to keep him alive; Coyle tries to trick the medic into loosening a restraint; Tran notices.

**Scene 82 - [SUSPENSE]**

Vance catches up with Archer, who almost shoots him. Archer is lost, doesn't know how to find Coyle. Vance knows the Pathet Lao camp from recon photos. He hands Archer night-vision goggles: "These should help even the odds."

**Scene 83 - [SUSPENSE]**

Coyle is tortured for the rotors' location. He tells them the rotors are gone — beyond their reach, already out of his hands — which is what he believes, though he has no confirmation Archer succeeded. The Pathet Lao don't believe him.

**Scene 84 - [SUSPENSE]**

Vance and Archer reach the camp and set Claymores along their escape route, then plan the rescue — very risky, good chance of failure, but Archer is set and Vance won't leave him. They prepare to assault.

**Scene 85 - [NON-ACTION]**

Tran receives a report that Coyle designed the gunships devastating the Trail; Hanoi wants him alive in Hanoi for interrogation. Tran orders his men to prepare to move out with Coyle; troops pour into the vehicles.

<!-- FINAL BATTLE -->
**Scene 86 - [ACTION]**

Sandy appears overhead and strafes trucks and guard towers with rockets, cannon, and napalm. NVA and Pathet Lao dive for cover as trucks and towers explode.

**Scene 87 - [ACTION]**

Archer and Vance seize the chaos and fight into the camp, night vision giving the edge while the enemy is blinded by fire and explosions.

**Scene 88 - [ACTION]**

Inside the camp, Archer fights through scattered guards toward the command tent — fire, smoke, confusion; the move column never forms. He spots where Coyle is being held.

**Scene 89 - [ACTION]**

Tran moves Coyle from the tent toward a remaining truck. Archer advances, firing his GAU-5/A at the Pathet Lao trying to stop him; Vance covers. Tran is wounded and forced to release Coyle. Archer grabs Coyle and helps him escape; Vance covers from the jungle at the perimeter.

**Scene 90 - [ACTION]**

Sandy makes another strafing run, sees Archer and Coyle escaping, breaks off, and destroys a different part of the camp — more Pathet Lao killed, better odds for the two men. The camp is flames and chaos.

**Scene 91 - [ACTION]**

Bounchanh, separated from his force in the chaos, fights his own battle through the burning camp — but survives, raging, as his column is scattered and the prisoner slips away. Refusing to let the American and his pilot walk out of his territory, he rallies his scattered men in the burning camp — cursing, threatening, dragging them into order — and drives them into the jungle in pursuit, taking the lead himself.

**Scene 92 - [ACTION]**

Coyle, Archer, and Vance run through the jungle. Vance stops to give covering fire, then runs on. Archer grabs a detonator; as Vance appears with the Pathet Lao on his tail firing, Archer hits it three times and a Claymore blows, ball bearings shredding the pursuers, killing six.

**Scene 93 - [ACTION]**

They press on. Vance drops and fires as the Pathet Lao reappear, buying time. Archer radios Bird Dog; Sandy joins and strafes the pursuers, who dive for cover. Vance waits, sets off the second Claymore — more shredded. The survivors balk until Bounchanh threatens them onward.

**Scene 94 - [ACTION]**

The Jolly Green hovers as Coyle and Archer approach. The crew lowers the penetrator; Coyle is too weak to hold on alone, so Archer rides up with him, wrapping himself around Coyle and gripping the cable for both of them as the hoist lifts them together into the bird. The penetrator lowers again. Vance, running with the Pathet Lao on his tail, takes a round in the leg and falls twenty meters short. Sandy drops his last napalm canister across the enemy's path, cutting them off. Vance limps to the penetrator and is hoisted in.

**Scene 95 - [ACTION]**

They take fire climbing out; some of it hits; the crew works the wounded as Dalton wrestles for altitude and clears the ridgeline. The Jolly Green flies to safety, escorted by Bird Dog and Sandy.

**Scene 96 - [NON-ACTION]**

The bird limps home; the crew's adrenaline crash; Vance working on Coyle on the cabin floor despite his own leg; few words, the weight of it.

**Scene 97 - [NON-ACTION]**

Back to NKP; touchdown; medics swarm the ramp; Vance finally lets go the breath he's held since Archer fell.

**Scene 98 - [NON-ACTION]**

Archer thanks Vance. Vance growls at him to never disobey an order again. "Yes, Master." "You're damn right."

**Scene 99 - [NON-ACTION]**

Coyle in the field hospital — he'll survive but never fly again; a quiet exchange with Archer; the half-finished whittled bird changes hands.

**Scene 100 - [NON-ACTION]**

Coda: Archer's two-year initiation compressed into days. He is no longer the man who couldn't sleep. Tran survived and will reappear; Bounchanh survives and holds his territory. The cost is counted; the man is changed.
